### **SEEK Algorithmic Mechanics**

#### **1. Local Contextual Aggregation**

$$L_i = \{s_{i-w+1}, \dots, s_i\} \quad \text{and} \quad R_i = \{s_{i+1}, \dots, s_{i+w}\}$$

$$l_i = \frac{1}{\vert{}L_i\vert{}} \sum_{s_j \in L_i} e_j \quad \text{and} \quad r_i = \frac{1}{\vert{}R_i\vert{}} \sum_{s_j \in R_i} e_j$$

* **Hyperparameter:** $w$ (Context window size).
* **📉 Impact:** A high $w$ smooths the overall semantic representation and ignores micro-syntactic changes. A low $w$ isolates the behavior of the immediate sentence.

#### **2. Semantic Distance ($\Delta_i$)**

$$\Delta_i = 1 - \cos(l_i, r_i)$$

* **Objective:** Quantify the thematic change (*topic shift*) via cosine distance at position $i$.

#### **3. Temporal Smoothing ($\tilde{\Delta}_i$)**

$$\tilde{\Delta}_i = \frac{1}{k} \sum_{j=i-\lfloor k/2 \rfloor}^{i+\lfloor k/2 \rfloor} \Delta_j$$

* **Hyperparameter:** $k$ (Sliding smoothing window size).
* **📉 Impact:** A high $k$ stabilizes the score curve by eliminating noise peaks (lexical false positives). A $k=1$ removes all smoothing and preserves micro-variations.

#### **4. Adaptive Threshold & Binary Decision**

$$\tau = \text{Percentile}(\tilde{\Delta}, p) \quad \text{and} \quad b_i = \mathbb{I}(\tilde{\Delta}_i \geq \tau)$$

* **Hyperparameter:** $p$ (Percentile of the distribution).
* **📉 Impact:** A $p$ close to 100% drastically restricts the number of boundaries (only major breaks pass through). A low $p$ multiplies segmentations by lowering the strictness requirement.

Our Adaptation: Reversed SEEK for Fine-Grained Attribution

#### 1. Infra-Chunk Segmentation by Regular Expression (Regex)

* **Method:** Systematic splitting of structural retrieved chunks at the character level: `.`, `;`, and `-`.
* **Objective:** Obtain ultra-fine text units (sentences or clauses) to precisely isolate the source information.

#### 2. Semantic Alignment: Atomic Fact ➔ Sub-Portion

* **SEEK Inversion:** Instead of measuring the rupture between two document windows ($l_i$ vs $r_i$), the algorithm computes the direct cosine similarity between the embedding of the atomic fact (generated by the response) and the chunk sub-portion smoothed by its neighborhood ($w$).

#### 3. Citation Decision Rule (Max-Pooling)

* **Logic:** If at least one infra-chunk unit exceeds the adaptive threshold $\tau$ calculated for a given fact ($b_i = 1$), the complete original chunk is validated and marked as cited for that fact.

#### 4. Search Space (Grid Search)

* **Percentile ($p$):** Exploration from $95\%$ to $99\%$ (0.1 step) to calibrate filter selectivity.
* **Context window ($w$):** Variations from $1$ to $4$ to adjust the semantic horizon around the regex clause.
* **Smoothing ($k$):** Variations from $1$ to $5$ to test inertia against local noise.

### **Overall Framework Pipeline**

* **1. Web Crawling:** Extract raw text from HTML pages and immediately convert each sentence into a **sentence embedding**.
* **2. Chunking (SEEK Module):** Use the sequence of sentence embeddings to detect local semantic changes and split the document at topic boundaries.
* **3. Retrieval:** Encode both the query (**claim embedding**) and the segmented passages (**chunk embeddings**) to retrieve the most relevant chunks using cosine similarity with a **FAISS** index.
* **4. Veracity Prediction:** Combine the query with the top retrieved chunks and use a **LoRA-enhanced LLM** to produce the final veracity prediction.
