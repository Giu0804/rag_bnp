# Grid Search Analysis (BART-MNLI)

### Expected Precision–Recall Trade-off

* Increasing the entailment threshold reduces false positives but also rejects valid evidence.
* Precision increases while recall decreases.

### Polarized Model Behavior

* Accuracy and F1-score remain nearly constant across thresholds.
* Most irrelevant chunks receive scores close to 0, while relevant chunks are clearly separated.

### Why 0.3–0.4 Is Optimal

* The model compares a short atomic fact with a long document chunk.
* Relevant evidence is diluted by surrounding sentences, limiting confidence scores.
* A threshold of **0.3–0.4** captures valid evidence while filtering out most noise.

### Key Insight

**The optimal threshold is determined by document-level evidence dilution rather than by model limitations.**

Ou encore plus académique :

# Key Findings from the Grid Search

* **Precision–Recall trade-off behaves as expected** when increasing the entailment threshold.
* **Accuracy and F1-score remain stable**, revealing a highly polarized score distribution.
* **Optimal threshold found at 0.3–0.4**, where relevant evidence emerges from document noise.
* **Higher thresholds (e.g., 0.8)** would discard a large proportion of valid supporting sources.
