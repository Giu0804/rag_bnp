# DIAPO 1 DATA

**Title: Data Panorama: Diversity and Complexity for RAG**

**Objective of the selected data:**
Go beyond simple single-paragraph extraction (Single-hop).
Provide corpora requiring the cross-referencing of multiple textual sources.

**Structural challenges of the corpora:**
Multi-hop reasoning: Linking facts that require connecting multiple documents together.
Volume and density: Handling very long contexts (up to 11,000 tokens) or large document collections (Top-100 passages).

**Our 4 working datasets:**
HotpotQA: Wikipedia-based corpus (113k Q/A) focused on navigating between related concepts.

MuSiQue: Question graphs (2 to 4 hops) built by assembling existing datasets.

QAMPARI (ALCE): Complex queries derived from knowledge graphs (Wikidata/Tables), producing answers in the form of entity lists.

RAGBench: Aggregation of 100k examples drawn from 5 industry sectors (legal, medical, finance, technical support, general knowledge).




--------------------------------------------------




**HotpotQA – Multi-hop reasoning and sentence-level evidence**

**Data source:** Text corpus based on the full English Wikipedia dump (October 2017). It specifically leverages the first paragraph of each article.

**Dataset construction:**
Creation of a hyperlink graph connecting Wikipedia articles in order to target “bridge entities.”
Questions are generated via crowdsourcing (Amazon Mechanical Turk) from pairs of connected paragraphs.

**Dataset characteristics:**
Strong supervision (supporting facts): the dataset includes the exact sentences used as evidence to produce the answer.

Comparison questions: includes questions requiring comparison between two distinct entities, sometimes involving arithmetic operations or yes/no reasoning.

**Key figures:**
113,000 question–answer pairs in total.
42% of questions require chain reasoning (inferring the first entity to find the second), and 27% are comparison questions.

**Relevant / Irrelevant management:**
The dataset includes a “Distractor” setting.
The input context is a noisy mixture containing 2 “gold” paragraphs (true positives with relevant information), mixed with 8 distractor paragraphs (false positives retrieved using tf-idf).
